Unit 3

Unified Modelling Language (UML)

Learning Outcomes

1. Recognise the syntax used to design UML models.
2. Distinguish between the UML models which are applicable at various stages of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC).
3. Develop UML models using open-source tools.

Artefacts

Discuss which UML models are most applicable at different stages of the Software Development Life Cycle.

During the planning stage, Activity Diagrams and Sequence Diagrams are the most useful, as they allow the planner to showcase the necessary functionality of the software. Activity Diagrams display the workflow, while Sequence Diagrams show the interaction between different elements.
During the Design Stage, a State Diagram should be made if applicable, following the concept behind planning. State Diagrams are useful as a guideline for implementation, making them crucial while designing.
After implementation, which does not require the creation of new diagrams, comes testing. During testing, as well as maintenance, creating State Diagrams based on the previously existing designed ones could be useful to convey any faults found in the state machine.

Reflection

This unit taught me more about the use of UML diagrams: both the creation of different types of them, as well as their use in stages of software's development.
