Unit 12

The Great Tanenbaum-Torvalds Debate Revisited

Learning Outcomes

  • Recommend a strategy around which methodology to adopt: monolithic or microservices.
  • Provide evidence to support your recommendation.
  • Explain how faceted data approaches work.

    • Meeting Notes

  • Date: 17 October 2024
  • Time (UK): 17:00-18:00
  • Venue: Google Meet
  • Attendees: Adriaan Joubert, Hristo Todorov, and Shraddha Gore
  • Agenda:
    • The meeting was scheduled to complete unit 11’s debate.
    • The forum had a message saying, “Torvalds has been proven wrong and it only took nearly thirty years. Microservices and microkernels are the future.”
    • The individual views about the statement were shared. We, as individuals and as a team, disagreed with the statement. This is because Linux is proof that a monolithic project can succeed and that many people can collaborate on such a project.

    • Reflection

    The main task of this unit was the completion of the final assignment: ePortfolio and Reflection. In my opinion, the reflection written is an accurate and well-presented piece which elaborates on my experiences with the module, focusing on the mistakes I have made and the steps I will take to avoid them in the future.
